Collegiate

I always had a passion for gaming. During my first year at UofT, I spent my year as a collegiate League of Legends player, representing the school as one of five players in the school's team. From my collegiate experience, I decided to take over the team as a coach and manager. This was a great opportunity to use both my gaming knowledge and organizational management knowledge (from my education at UofT) to practical use.

The competitive video gaming industry has been evolving at a rapid pace over the years, and the collegiate E-Sports (meaning, competitive video gaming) has been growing along side it. For the game League of Legends, each year each university chooses a team of five talented players to take part in the RiotGames (the creator of the game and the tournament) tournament, named CLOL. The first place prize pool is $10,000 per player as scholarship, with varying prize pool for teams placed below. Separated by conferences, UofT took part in the Eastern conference and played against rouhgly 80 university teams each year. In 2020, the UTM Eagles have made 7th out of the 80 teams in the tournament, reaching play-offs and quarter-finals. The team has won roughly $700 in scholarships for each memeber. This is a phenomenal accomplishment that the school, the team, and myself are extremely proud of.

My time with the team allowed me to showcase my knowledge on organizational behaviour and my managerial skills for a 'small-sized team.' In particular, conflict resolution and finding an effective communication channel were the biggest challenege and opportunities in this endeavor. I am particularly proud of my growth in building interpersonal relationships with the players and learning how to coach for the player's mental well-being.

For more information on CLOL, please visit the official tournament website. For more information on E-Sports and collegiate, feel free to watch the video on the right. For more information regarding the team and the school's club, feel ree to check our official Facebook page.

My responsibilities and roles

# Responsibilities
1 Investigate, analyze and organize players' available hours and form an official schedule for team and individual practices.
2 Ensure players are on-time for practices and tournament games.
3 Set-up the team's practice regiment and ensure it is followed.
4 Find and contact practice partners (i.e. other university teams) to set practices against teams that match our team's skill-level.
5 Facilitate team discussions after practices in an organized, precise, and cooperative manner.
5 Act as the official 'ambassador' when contacting other universities or the tournament officials.
6 Resolve any player-conflicts in a positive and constructive manner.
7 Collaborate with the university's E-Sports club for .
8 Enrol the team into official and local tournaments.
9 Research, analyze, and learn from the professional e-sports teams, and apply the knowledge through coaching.
