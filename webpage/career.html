~1996 Pre-NBA Kobe

Born in Philadelphia, Kobe started playing basketball at the age of three. Kobe's favourite team was the Lakers. Kobe spent his youth practicing basketball, watching NBA videos sent by his grandfather to study, and watching European films aobut sports. Bryant's high school basketball career at Lower Merion High School in Ardmore (in Philadelphia subrub) was spectacular. While Kobe's freshman year finsihed with a 4-20 record, the next three years experienced Kobe's talents prosper. The team managed a 77-13 record, with Kobe averaging 31.1 points, 10.4 rebounds, 5.2 assists, 3.9 blocks and 2.3 steals. Kobe was known to swap between all five positions throughout his high school career, which makes this even more astounding.

Bryant even led his team to their first state championship in 53 years, with astounding averages of 30.8 points, 12 rebounds, 6.5 assists, 4 steals, and 3.8 blocks. Kobe finished his high school career with awards, such as the Naismith High School Player of the Year, Gatorade Men's National Basketball Player of the Year, and the McDonald's All-American. Kobe decided to enter directly into the NBA after high school at the age of 17, being one of the six players to ever do so in NBA history.

Kobe Bryant in the NBA

1996-1999

The NBA Draft and Adjustments

  • Bryant was drafted by the Hornets and traded to the Lakers at the age of 17 for $3.5 million
  • Debuted in Summer Pro League with 25 points
  • Scored 36 points in the finale for Summer Pro League
  • Youngest player ever to play in the NBA
  • Won 1997 Slam Dunk Contest (Youngest Winner)
  • Reached Western Conference Semifinals and lost to Utah Jazz 4:1
  • Soon emerged into a premier guard in the NBA, becoming the youngest NBA All-Star

1999-2002

Three-peat

  • Received coaching from Phil Jackson
  • Won 3 championships consecutively (2000, 2001, 2002) with O'Neal
  • Won 67 games in the 1999-2000 season
  • 25 points, 11 rebound, 7 assist, 4 block in the G7 Finals of the WC against the Trail Blazers
  • Scored the winning shot against the Pacers in the 2000 Finals to give Lakers their first championship since 1988
  • Averaged 29.4 points, 7.3 rebounds, 6.1 assists per game in the 2000 playoffs
  • Won his first All-Star MVP trophy in 2002 after a 31-point performance in Philadelphia

2002-2004

Coming short

  • Averaged 40.6 points in nine consecutive games in 2002
  • Lost in the WC semi-finals against the Spurs (2003)
  • Career-high stat improvements with averages of 6.9 rebounds, 5.9 assists, and 2.2 steals per game
  • Missed some games due to his sexual assault case
  • Made two buzzer-beaters to win against the Trail Blazers in the final season game to win the Pacific Division title
  • Lost to the Pistons in the NBA Finals
  • His duo O'Neal was signed to Miami, while he remained with the Laker's

2004-2007

Upsets

  • Bryant was NBA's second highest scorer with 27.6 points per game
  • Criticism from prior coach Jackson and support staffs impacted Bryant's public image
  • Bryant made up with coach Jackson, and the two brought Lakers back to play-offs
  • Kobe Bryant outscores the entire Mavericks team (62-61) by the fourth quarter
  • Kobe reached a career-high 81 points victory against the Raptors (2006)
  • Kobe Bryant changes his jersey number back to the iconic #24
  • Unfotunate play-off losses against Pheonix Suns in both 2006 and 2007

2007-2010

To the Top

  • In 2007, Kobe bacme the youngest player to reach 20,000 points (29 years-old)
  • Bryant became the League MVP by 2008
  • 2007-2008 season finished with 57-25
  • Lost in the 2008-NBA finals to the Celtics
  • Won the 2009 NBA finals against Orlando Magic
  • Kobe named as the 2009 NBA finals MVP
  • In 2009, Kobe bacme the youngest player to reach 25,000 points (31 years old)

2010-2013

Injuries

  • Kobe became one of seven players to reach 25,000 points, 5,000 rebounds, and 5,000 assists
  • Went through several surgeries for his knee and nose
  • In 2012, Kobe became the youngest player to score 30,000 points (34 years old)
  • Kobe became the first player to reach 47 points, 8 rebounds, 5 assists, 4 blocks, and 3 steals in an NBA game
  • Tore his achilles tendon against Golden State Warriors after 7 consecutive 40-minute games
  • Lakers, weak without Kobe, lost against the Spurs
  • NewYorkTimes called Kobe's 2013 performance perhaps one of his best

2013-2015

Injuries

  • Kobe remained the league's highest-paid player
  • Kobe suffered a fracture in his left knee
  • Laker had poor showing due to Kobe's absence due to rehab
  • Bryant returned to the league in 2014, becoming the third all-time leading scorer, beating Michael Jordan
  • Kobe performed great in the early season, but was taken out after his injuries grew worse
  • Even after his right shoulder injury, Kobe played some games shooting, dribbling, and passing almost exclusively using his left hand
  • With Kobe out due to injuries, Lakers had another poor showing with a 21-61 season score

2015-2016

The Final Season

  • Bryant suffered from a calf injury post recovery
  • Due to his poor health, Bryant showed one of his worst showings in his career, shooting 1 for 14 against the Warriors in November 24, 2015
  • Bryant announced his retirement on November 29, 2015, due to his poor health
  • Bryant had his final game against his hometown, Philadelphia 76ers, on December 1, 2015; a 103-91 loss
  • Bryant changed his role to small forward, even making 38 points against the Timberwolves on February 3, 2016
  • Even with his injuries and comparatively poor performance, Kobe was chosen as the All-Star player for the year 2016
  • In his last NBA game, Bryant scored a season-high 60 points against Utah Jazz, leading the Lakers to victory (101-96)

2006-2012

Kobe in Nationals

  • Kobe's outstanding performance and fame opened plenty of opportunties for him in national team basketball
  • Kobe was a member of the United States national team in 2007
  • Participated and won the USA FIBA Americas Championship in 2007 by 10-0
  • Averaged 15.3 points, 2.9 assists, 2.0 rebounds, and 1.6 steals per game
  • Won the Gold medal in the 2008 Olympics, averaging 15 points, 2.8 rebounds and 2.1 assists
  • Won the 2012 Summer Olympics gold medal
  • Kobe retired from the nationals team with a career record of 26-0, 3 gold medals in three tournaments
