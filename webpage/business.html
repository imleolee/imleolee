Kobe Bryant Off the Court

Endorsements

Kobe was well-known for his major endorsement deals with major corporations. His first endorsement was with Adidas; a six-year contract deal for $48 million. His other deals were involved with Coca-Cola, Sprite, McDonalds, Nutella, and even video game companies, such as Nintendo. His biggest work with Nike is considered his Nike Zoom series. His endorsement deals and his basketball career made him the third highest-paid athlete in the world in 2010, and the fifth highest-paid athlete in 2013.

Kobe went beyond His other businesses

Kobe took part in business ventures, film and TV appearances. Kobe created Kobe Inc. in the hopes to grow brands in the sports industry, managing to raise his stakes to $200 million once Coca-Cola purchased Kobe Inc's minority stake.

Kobe took quite an interest in the film industry. He first launched Granity Studios, a media company involved in film, teleivison shows, and novel production. His film, "Dear Basketball," made him the first African-American to win the Academy Award for Best Animated Short Film. The film also won Sports Emmy Award and Annie Award for the Best Animated Short Subject film.

Kobe was a celebrity, and he appeared as "Kobe Bryant the Celebrity" on multiple TV shows, such as Moesha(1996; his debut acting), Arli$$, Sister Sister, and All That, a Nikelodean sketch.
